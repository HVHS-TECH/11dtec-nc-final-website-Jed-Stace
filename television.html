Music is one of the most powerful invisible forces in storytelling. Before a single word of dialogue is spoken, the score tells you how to feel whether you're safe, in danger, falling in love, or about to laugh. Composers and directors work together to craft soundscapes that guide your emotional response without you even noticing.

Types of effects:

From the thundering brass of a villain's entrance to the delicate piano notes of a farewell scene, film music falls into several key categories. Diegetic music exists within the world of the story (a character playing guitar, a radio in the background), while non-diegetic music is heard only by the audience. Leitmotifs recurring musical themes tied to a character or idea are another powerful tool, made famous by composers like John Williams and Hans Zimmer.
